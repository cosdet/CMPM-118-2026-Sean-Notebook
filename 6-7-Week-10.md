## What did I Gain?

I gained a lot of experience this quarter compared to last quarter. This quarter I learned even more about the GitHub/Git ecosystem and how making changes to an open-source project like Twizzler goes. Especially things like making my own fork, creating my own branch off that fork, making sure my submodules and main branch are up-to-date or at certain commits to make things work, etc. I also learned the intimate details of the Twizzler terminal and display systems (and the lack thereof) and began to learn how the toolchain works. I also learned that maybe standing desks are overrated and Twizzlers are actually quite snackable...

There was a moment that genuinely changed how I think about development while I was wokring on Femto. It was the moment I had to re-bootstrap `mlibc` everytime I made a change to it, which I'm sure I mentioned at every all-team meeting, haha. However, because of this, I began to think about how useful development tools really are and how beneficial it can be to make them as efficient as possible to prevent development burnout. This is something I hope to bring with me in future work in my personal projects and even on Twizzler.

Finally, to touch on a question posed to me by the TAs: Did this class meet the goals set out in the beginning? I think... yes! It was set out from the beginning that the TAs would, for the second+ cohort, give assistance and check in on progresss towards a final goal. There were many times where TA Max and Surendra helped me out of a technical or creative rut, or gave me reasssurance on a risky move/command before I executed on it.

## Feedback

I thought this quarter was much much much better and well-structured compared to last quarter. I really liked the weekly notebooks. They gave me a chance to just deposit all I learned/found interesting during the week into one place that I can look back on later. There was actually a time where I was revisting my old notebooks just to see which files I was looking at earlier in the quarter. The paper readings we had every few weeks were, I thought, somewhat distracting from working on my research project, but I understand their purpose in the lab and wouldn't make any major changes to the system. 

The 3 hour lab sessions were, I think, pretty long and unecessary for most people this quarter. This is simply because for the first cohort, a lot of the work is individual and doesn't really encourage group work or discussion. Even though the lab time is designed to give them time to discuss amongst themselves in case they don't understand a feature or bug, I feel like, from what I've heard and seen regarding the practice Rust exercises, that a lot of the bugs and questions are easily explained by reading the instructions of the exercises or the Rust book. For the second cohort, it definitely would've been nice to have the other labmates stay for longer to just see what they're working on and chat. Outside of the lab though, I would say a lot of my work was done at home even if I did end up staying in the lab for the full six hours every week. This is simply because it's where my snacks, monitor, mouse, and keyboard are. However, with the changes being made to the lab space currently, I've been working more and spending more time in the lab :)

I thought the research project structure was actually scoped pretty well. I really liked picking something from Daniel's list of features/ideas to be implemented and being able to claim it as your feature for the quarter. This made it easy to check in with other second cohort members, as we would be working on separate, but still Twizzler-related, features. On the topic of A.I, I think A.I. use is fine after the first cohort. I'd probably, as the cohort grows in size, have some system in place to ensure that A.I. is being used sparingly or is checked with scrutiny to ensure that slop code isn't pushed to main that damages other features or will be a pain to edit/figure out later down the line. As of right now, Daniel is the only line of defense in this regard whenver a P.R. is made.

Otherwise, thank you TAs Surendra and Max for volunteering your time this quarter and helping this lab grow, hope to see you guys around in the Fall!
